What is geographic data?
Geographic data represents things that have a location.
This can include physical things like roads, rivers, and cities.
It can also include ephemeral phenomena, like rainfall rates, 911 calls, and weather patterns.
Geographic data can also capture nuances of locations, like the slope on the side of a mountain.
(But we'll get to that.)
How do we use geographic information?
Typically, geographic information is used for two things: visualization and analysis.
It is often used for both!
There are many types of geographic data, used by many different types of software, web tools, and libraries, for many different use cases.
